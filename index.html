WHY AUPRC IS THE PRIMARY METRIC

The classes are steeply imbalanced — CAFOs appear in 29.3% of training scenes while Mines appear in just 2.1%, and 40.2% are negatives.

Under imbalance, accuracy and AUROC are dominated by the majority class and flatter the model. Area under the precision–recall curve (AUPRC) reflects performance on the rare positive class directly, so we rank every experiment by macro-averaged AUPRC.

RGB-ONLY IS ALMOST ENOUGH

NAIP RGB alone already reaches ~0.75 macro AUPRC — within ~0.10 of the full multi-sensor champion (0.852), and well above the paper's 0.558.

Most of the accuracy is available from a single RGB channel. Where collection cost or latency matters, the extra NIR and Sentinel channels can be dropped with only a small accuracy trade-off.

THE CHALLENGE

One model for every methane source.

Anthropogenic methane is the leading driver of rising atmospheric methane. Mapping the infrastructure behind it is a prerequisite for cutting emissions — our contribution to worldwide sustainability efforts.

The task was to beat the METER-ML benchmark. Rather than a family of per-sensor models, we converged on a single network that fuses NAIP, Sentinel-1 and Sentinel-2 imagery and predicts all six facility classes at once — outperforming the paper's per-class expert ensemble.
